Air Balloon

A tea light holder that uses heat to spin a paper hot air balloon. The stand can be quickly assembled and has a cavity which securely holds standard size tea lights.

Paper Airplane Pop Up Card

A pop up of the paper airplane from the movie The Wind Rises. The airplane rises using the floating plane mechanic.

Totoro Pop Up Card

A pop up of Totoro from the movie My Neighbor Totoro. The card is made using the slice form method and rises using a disconnected V-fold.

Jiji In Cage Pop Up Card

A pop up of JiJi trapped in a cage from the movie Kiki's Delivery Service. The card is made using the slice form method and rises using a cross section parallel fold.

Spirited Away Bridge Pop Up Book

A hard cover pop up of the bridge from the movie Spirited Away. The pop up uses a system of mechanics I designed which are all hidden underneath the page. This innovation allows for figures far away from the center (the two characters on the right) to be able to rise with no visible supports.

The system also makes it possible for the bridge to rise perpendicular to the viewer and for the ends of the bridge to curve into the page.

The book also features a collapsing spine, custom artwork and multi-layered embossing on the cover.

Memory Pop Up Book

A pop up recreation of a couple on a Boston sidewalk. My most complex piece, the pop up features a tree that rotates off center from the page, figures that rise both perpendicular and diagonal to the viewer, figures that rise away from the center of the page, and multi-layered architecture.

The pop up uses a system I designed which allows for all the figures to rise with no visible supports.

Adidas Pop Up

A pop up of the Adidas logo. The pop up is made of multiple, disconnected floating planes and features the Adidas leaf on the front.

Paper Violin

A playable violin made from paper and popsicle sticks. The project was an independent study advised by Professor Steve Bowdin, completed at the University of Connecticut in the Spring 2019 Semester.

My goal with this project was to make a violin using only basic materials (paper, popsicle sticks, glue), as traditional violin making requires many expensive materials and tools. The design and build process made use of 3D modeling software, CAD, and the construction took around 200 hours.

Paper Embossment System

A system I designed used to create precise multi-layered embossments. Traditional embossing either requires hand carving linoleum blocks, or an entire embossing machine with metal plates. I designed this system so I could create precise embossments using only paper.
